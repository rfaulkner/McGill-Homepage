Wikiwind!


The last two weeks have been an absolute whirlwind! The Wikimedia Foundation fundraiser kicked off on the 12th and the entire fundraising team has been pushing hard to maximize the effectiveness of our campaign. My role in this has been to collect and scrutinize the ongoing torrent of data running in from donations and site traffic generated by the fundraiser. Today we passed the $4 million mark!!

On the data side we've come a very long way in ten days; from manually pulling what we need out of server logs and open source databases that keep track of our donations (in a muddled fashion). We stand today with the emergence of an automated system that pulls the data as rapidly it flows into our servers, and organizes it concisely enough that meaningful trends can be extracted along with the statistical validity of our data. We can now really start asking the more interesting questions about the data as it's accessible outside of only its rawest form. I've put together the following page in an effort to start in helping us answer some of those questions while inspiring new, more interesting ones.

We seem to be at a point where we can take a breath and examine what is actually happening. By that I mean that the purpose of the campaign is not only to fundraise but also to gather statistics on how to effectively engage our readers and our community. This will hopefully become the start of a process that transforms Wikipedia into something that people not only seek out as a source of knowledge, but pushes those readers - even in the smallest sense - to actively contribute to the existing content.
